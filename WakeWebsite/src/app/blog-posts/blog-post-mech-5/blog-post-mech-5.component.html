What does it take to make a WAKE? Part 3: Bad vibrations, repairing hole – February 10, 2021 Fabrication Team

From the first day of work, when the slots were cut into the frame, the accidental hole through the frame needed to be repaired. While the hole would be out of sight on the final product, it was simple enough to fix at this time. Two pieces were used to fill this gap, a plug drilled using a hole saw bit out of the extra cherry wood, and a very slim shim cut off of another extra piece of wood. The plug is shown in Figure 2 below, with the best plug was filed and fitted to the hole, and the shim piece can be seen in Figure 3.

With this done, the pieces could be glued into place, and can be seen in Figure 4. As this process was going on, the excess glue was carefully wiped away to reduce the amount of sanding required after it dried. In the end, the hole was fixed quite nicely on the backside, making it appear more like a knot that was replaced instead of a hole as can be seen in Figure 5.
