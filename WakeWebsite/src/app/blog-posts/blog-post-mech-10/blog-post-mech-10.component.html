What does it take to make a WAKE? Part 8: Stain, Mirror, Braces, and gluing – February 27, 2021 Fabrication Team

With the all the major components fit and holes/slots made in the frame and the enclosure it was finally time to stain the project. All of the components were carefully removed and stored in a separate location, while both the frame and the enclosure were completely sanded down with medium grade and then fine grade sandpaper. With this down, the cherry wood stain as shown in Figure 2 could be applied. However, the back side of the frame was left unstained at this time as the final assembly called for the two pieces to be glued together. In total two coats of stain were applied over a 48 hour period.

While the stain was drying, it was finally time to created the mirrored surface of the system. First, using a packing knife the clear 18" acrylic sheet was cut down to size in order to fit in the front of the enclosure on top of the monitor. Once this was down the reflective mirror could be applied to it. Using soap, a spray bottle of water, and a squeegee the reflective film was carefully applied to the clear surface of the acrylic. In Figure 3, both the tools used and the final reflective surface of the two-way acrylic mirror can be seen.

One final mechanical change to the mirror system, came after a dry run to fit in all the electronics, and was the addition of brackets to the power bar that would have to be held vertically within the enclosure in order to fit all of the wires properly. In Figure 4, these brackets can be seen placed specifically avoid interfering with the wires, and later bent into place to hold the power bar tightly.

With all these steps complete it was time for the moment of truth, gluing the assembly together. A quick check of the monitor almost caused a heart attack or stroke, as it didn’t turn on. But a check of the power ribbon revealed it had accidentally been pulled loose. It was fixed back into place, along with the monitor and the acrylic two-way mirror on top of it before the frame was held on top. As gluing together the final assembly would be difficult to do, we spent 30 measuring and squaring each side of the frame with the enclosure until we were within 132" at each corner and in each direction in the x-y plane of the mirror. Once we were satisfied, lines were drawn in pencil on the backside of the unstained frame shown in Figure 5. Glue was then applied to the top of the enclosure, and another half-hour was spent positioning the frame on the front along with installing the required clamps in place, as shown in Figure 6.
