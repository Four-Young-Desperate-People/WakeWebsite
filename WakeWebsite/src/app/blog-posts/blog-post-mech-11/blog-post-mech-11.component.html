What does it take to make a WAKE? Part 9: Finish, Wire Management, and Installed on wall – March 6, 2021 Fabrication Team

After letting the glue cure, the clamps were removed from the frame and 3 coats of finish were applied to give the mirror a nice gloss look. This gloss appearance can be best seen in Figure 2 below.

Once the finishing coats were completed and dried, the full set of wires (excluding the LED cables) were carefully placed into the back of the enclosure. Proper wire management was completed to ensure everything fit nicely into the back. Any extra long cables had the excess looped and taped out of the way, while buttons and ports that needed to be accessible were arranged with enough room to do so as shown in Figure 3 and 4.

Now with all the major components in place, stained and finished, a couple of photos of the mirror were taken as well as testing the monitors ability to display different screens through the mirror. In Figure 5 below one can see both the highly reflective nature of the mirror as well as how well images played on the monitor are displayed through the two-way acrylic mirror. One final test was to mount the French cleat onto the wall and see if everything held up. Further photos of this are shown in Figure 6, where the mirror was mounted for nearly 2 weeks before being handed over to the software team for the next steps in integration. In all, the system weighed about 25 lbs which was very close to what was expected for it.
