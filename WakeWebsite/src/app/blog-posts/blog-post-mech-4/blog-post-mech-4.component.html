What does it take to make a WAKE? Part 2: Breaking the molds, Aluminum Channels and holes – February 3, 2021 Fabrication Team

The last step of the previous week was to glue the frames together in wood molds. Hence, the first step this week was to remove the enclosure and frame from the molds. Upon doing so, one could see the residue of the glue in the area around the mitre joints on both the frame and enclosure, Figure 2 below for example. Therefore, the excess glue was sanded off the joints before continuing with any further construction.

With that completed, the next step was to cut the main components for the LED light system on the front frame of the mirror. Using a wooden mitre box and a hand saw, each piece was cut down to size in order to fit into the previously cut slots, as shown in Figure 3 below. The opal LED light diffuser was soft plastic, and care needed to be taken not to scratch the front surface. On the other hand, the LED aluminum channel was stronger and after being cut had a much rougher edge that required the ends to be deburred for the end caps to fit on cleanly. To deburr the ends, the shop grinders was used as shown in Figure 4.

Before fitting the LED components into the frame’s slots, one final step was required. While the router straight bit had removed the majority of the cherry wood in the slot, at both ends there were rounded ends that needed to be squared using a chisel and hammer. In Figure 5 below, these ends can be seen before, during, and after chiseling as well as the components fitting snugly into place.

The remaining step was to drill holes through the aluminum channel and cherry wood of the frame so that a 5-wire 18 AWG cable could be fed to the LED strips. The drill press was used for this again, as it allowed the hole to be placed close to the bottom end of the slots, and made easy work as the cherry wood was quite hard. The results of this task can be seen in Figure 6 below.

With all these tasks completed the end caps, opal diffuser, and aluminum channel could be placed into each slot of the front frame. The initial fit was really good and didn’t require the installation of the mounting brackets in order to hold the LED components in place as shown in Figure 7.
