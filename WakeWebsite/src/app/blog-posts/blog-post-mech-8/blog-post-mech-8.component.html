What does it take to make a WAKE? Part 6: Fitting in and form fitting – February 19, 2021 Fabrication Team

With all the major mechanical parts of WAKE mirror system completed, it was time to start fitting in the major electrical components, starting first with the stripped-down monitor screen. However, while the screen fit in loosely along the vertical length as shown in Figure 2, it can be seen in Figure 3 that the width of the enclosure was approximately 332" to short to fit the screen in place. Obviously, not accounting for tolerances on the enclosure drawings and in the redesign of the system for the smaller monitor led to this discrepancy being missed until after major components were built. It certainly was a disappointing development as there was not enough wood to rebuild the enclosure and significant time had already been sunk into the construction of the original enclosure. Despite this, it was decided to cut a 18” wide, ¾” deep ridge into both of the front sides of the enclosure to have enough room for the monitor and acrylic mirror to fit in flush to the front of the enclosure. In Figure 4, a new routing fence can be seen setup on the routing table, as well as the ridge cut into the front of the enclosure.

The monitor was then placed into the enclosure again, as shown in Figure 5, and this time it fit in nicely while leaving enough room for the acrylic mirror to be installed on top later. Next, brackets were placed in the back of the enclosure, as shown in Figure 6. The brackets at the top and bottom were to hold the monitor level, as the back of the monitor had several different features that would cause the mirror to be tilted otherwise. The other bracket at the bottom of the enclosure is to serve as the mounting location of the Raspberry Pi Board, so that it is isolated from the other electrical components.

Finally, the position of the Pi board on the top mounting bracket was marked onto the wood and holes drilled to allow the nylon threaded screws to fasten it to the board on a future day, as shown in Figure 7.
